Honorable Mentions

Skansen

The first ever open-air museum in the world on Royal Djurgården in 1891.

Stockholm Medieval Museum

Here you can track the history of the city from its foundation in the 13th century to the 16th century at the end of the medieval period.

The Royal Palace

With more than 600 rooms Stockholm’s Royal Palace is up there with the largest palaces in Europe.

Fotografiska

There are four high-profile exhibitions staged at Fotografiska per year, along with 20 smaller shows, so no two visits will ever be the same.

ABBA: The Museum

The museum brims with ABBA memorabilia and clever touches that fans will be wild for.

Top 3 places to Visit

These 3 places are places that most tourist visit when in Stockholm. Fun for everyone and even educational!

Vasa Museum

The Vasa was a 64-gun warship that went down on its first voyage in 1628. It remained in the deep until 1961 when it was lifted to the surface and slowly and painstakingly restored.

  • Experience: Visiting the Vasa Museum is a learning experience for everyone!
  • Cost: Free for kids, 170.00kr for adults ($19.72)
  • More Information: Here

Junibacken

Based on the stories of children’s author Astrid Lindgren, Junibacken is a theme park aimed at young readers.

  • Experience: Fun for everyone! Especially the children.
  • Cost: Free for kids, Children: 2-15 years 165 ($19.14) SEK, Adult: 195 SEK ($22.61)
  • More Information: Here

Mojang Studios

Mojang is the game studio responsible for creating Minecraft. A popular sandbox game that was released in 2011. They are also responsible for Minecraft Dungeons.

  • Experience: This location is a work place and at times holds private events.
  • Cost: Minecraft is $26.95
  • More Information: Here
